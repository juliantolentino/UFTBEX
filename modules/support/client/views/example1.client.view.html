Design : Algonquian Wigwam

Grade(s) : 3 - 3

Ask

Describe your goal:
Improve the design of an Algonquian Wigwam

What's the major problem or underlying question?
Everything was tied together. Saplings have to be cut and dragged

How can your students solve this problem?

Camping tent design

List your constraints:
Need saplings, birch bark or woven mats. Need smoke hole and door

Imagine

What are some solutions? Brainstorm ideas.

Plan

Draw a diagram or any other representation of your project.

List the materials you need.

  • Thin coffee sticks
  • String
  • Paper squares

Essential Sketch & Details:

Mathematics:

Main Idea:

Looking for patterns in shape and purpose

Essential Details:

Students will see a pattern between dimensions in native dwelling and use these to improve design

What students will do:

Sort shapes of dwellings, investigate patterns

Essential Details:

Students will identify shapes of dwellings and patterns. Then they will use dimensional constraints to design a dwelling

Linked Math Standard(s):

MAFS.K12.MP.7.1
Science:

Main Idea:

The practice of science

Essential Details:

Students will investigate dwelling design and tie design to habitat of native people. Students will pose a question about dwelling design.

What students will do:

Create a question, collect data, evaluate data

Essential Details:

Students will ask a question related to dwelling structure - Students will use engineering design to help answer this question

Linked Science Standard(s):

SC.3.N.1.3
Literature:

Main Idea:

Compare different Native American dwellings

Essential Details:

Students read if you lived with the ... (Hopi, Sioux, Iroquois, Indians of the Northwest Coast). Focus on housing.

What students will do:

Make a comparison chart

Essential Details:

Students will read informational texts to determine types of dwellings of native culture

Linked Literature Standard(s):

LAFS.3.RI.3.9
Social Studies:
Comparing Cultures of Native People

Essential Details:

Students study habitats, environment and cultures of native people in the Americas

What students will do:

Make a chart to identify natural resources, habitat and culture

Essential Details:

Students will use SS texts and readings to identify resources and climate constraints in dwelling design

Linked Social Studies Standard(s):

SS.3.G.4.3
How will students communicate findings?
Students will design a poster to present findings
